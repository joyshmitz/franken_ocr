Shovell had left England in May with belated instructions to endeavour to intercept the French fleet, bound from Brest to Toulon; to provide for the safety of the trade; to convoy certain vessels to Lisbon; and, if he missed the enemy, to reinforce Sir George Rooke.
The Admirals at once held a council of war and decided to look for the French in the Mediterranean. Rear-Admiral Byng was first, however, detached to Cadiz to effect an exchange of prisoners. He rejoined Sir George off Cape Trafalgar on June 29th; but
